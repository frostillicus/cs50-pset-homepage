What are Solar Eclipses?

Solar eclipses are caused by the Moon passing directly between the Sun and Earth. Due to the extreme size distance between the Sun and Moon, two shadows will be created: the penumbra, a large, lighter shadow, and the umbra, which is a smaller, darker shadow in the center of the penumbra.

Warning: Never look at an eclipse without the proper protection. Always use eclipse glasses or solar filters.

Note that the diagram below is not to scale. In reality, the distances are far larger, and the Sun is much larger than the Earth.

People who are in the umbra will be treated to a dramatic eclipse where the Sun is almost completely blocked by the Moon. However, the Sun's corona will be visible as a luminous aura around the moon (you can see the corona in the image at the top of this page).

People within the penumbra will see the Sun partially blocked by the Moon.
